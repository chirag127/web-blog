Welcome to **oriz-blog** — the long-form writing arm of the
[chirag127/oriz](https://github.com/chirag127/oriz) family of static
sites. Most of what I publish here is engineering work I'd otherwise
forget: notes on shipping small things, debugging stories with the
fix written down so I don't repeat the search, and the occasional
opinionated take on a tool I've used long enough to have one.

## What you'll find

Three buckets, roughly:

- **Engineering notebooks.** Architecture decisions, debugging
  walk-throughs, and the kind of "I just spent four hours on this"
  post that's mostly for me but might save you the same four hours.
- **Open-source ships.** Whenever I push a new oriz-family site
  (eleven of them and counting), the cross-post engine
  ([oriz-omnipost](/posts/cross-post-engine/)) pings here and the
  newsletter and dev.to and Bluesky in one go.
- **Books and finance.** The non-engineering side of my
  reading. Less frequent, more digested.

Every post lives as an `.mdx` file in this repo's
`src/content/posts/` directory — no headless CMS, no admin login,
no second source of truth. See the
[markdown-in-repo-only decision](https://github.com/chirag127/oriz/blob/main/knowledge/decisions/architecture/cms-markdown-in-repo-only.md)
for the why.

## Code samples come from elsewhere

Per the family-wide
[code-embed lock (Batch 6)](https://github.com/chirag127/oriz/tree/main/knowledge/decisions),
runnable code goes into a **StackBlitz** or **CodePen** project, or
a **GitHub Gist** for non-runnable snippets, and gets embedded back
into the post. This blog post embed pattern keeps the MDX file
short, the runtime example always-up-to-date, and the JS bundle
out of the static build.

## How to read

The chrome here is intentionally minimal. The header has a search
box that hits the family-wide MultiSearch (looks across every site
in oriz, not just this blog). The footer has the three feeds —
[`/rss.xml`](/rss.xml), [`/atom.xml`](/atom.xml),
[`/feed.json`](/feed.json) — pick whichever your reader prefers.
Comments are at the bottom of each post, gated behind a "Load
comments" click so the iframe doesn't ship until you ask for it.

If something here helps you, the best thank-you is a star on
[chirag127/oriz](https://github.com/chirag127/oriz) or a reply on
[Bluesky](https://bsky.app/profile/chirag127.bsky.social).
